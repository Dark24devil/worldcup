The Legacy of the World Cup

Since its inception in 1930, the FIFA World Cup has been the ultimate stage for footballing greatness. From the inaugural victory of Uruguay to the modern era of tactical brilliance, it has defined sporting excellence across generations.

Legendary figures like Pelé, the only three-time winner, and Diego Maradona, whose "Hand of God" and "Goal of the Century" became part of sporting lore, have graced this tournament. As we look toward 2026, we honor the icons who turned football into a global religion.

Led by a generation of superstars and tactical innovators, the French national team continues to redefine modern football with speed, precision, and flair. As they cross the Atlantic, their quest for a third star remains the ultimate goal.
